The world was too empty and the edges were misbehaving. Both fixed, and neglected garbage got dangerous.

- **Shrank the play area** so there's less dead space between the action.
- Fixed the **screen-wrap seam**, where objects would visually lurch when crossing the edge of the world.
- Garbage now drifts toward other garbage. Leave enough of it uncollected and **twelve pieces coalesce into a Hunter satellite**, with an ominous warning cue even if it happens off-screen. That's Kessler syndrome, and yes, it's your fault.
