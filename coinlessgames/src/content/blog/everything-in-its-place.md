A readability-and-structure pass, mostly. The HUD stops hiding things, the menus stop repeating themselves, and the galaxy's least-glamorous garbage hauler picks up a couple of new problems to solve.

- **The HUD tells the whole story now.** Every powerup — Triple Shot, Rapid Shot, Magnet, and the Scoop — keeps a permanent spot on the display. When you don't have one, its ring sits there muted with a zero, so an empty slot reads as "go find this," not "wait, what used to be here?" A small change that quietly removes a lot of guesswork.
- **The Scoop is a gauge you can read at a glance.** Its ring is now cut into five wedges that fill as you build the upgrade and drain as you take hits. Losing a wedge lands harder than watching a number tick down ever did — which is exactly the point.
- **Saucers get sharper the deeper you go.** UFO fire felt just right on level one, so rather than touch that, their accuracy now climbs gradually across waves on its own curve. Early levels stay friendly; late runs make you genuinely wary of the saucers. The ramp is intentionally gentle — slow-building pressure, not a wall you smack into.
- **A trophy for the haul that shouldn't make it.** Getting a full maximum tow to the recycler in one piece is brutally hard, so pulling it off now unlocks a lifetime achievement and a **MAX HAUL** celebration. If you've ever threaded a full chain past a waking Hunter, you've earned the fanfare.
- **An optional safety net, with a price.** A new Difficulty setting lets your shields snap on automatically the instant you take a hit at critical hull. That's strong, so it isn't free — every automatic save costs 500 points, and we'll keep balancing that knob in the next set.
- **Menus that finally agree with themselves.** The Menu, Options, and Paused dialogs had drifted apart, with Achievements somehow living in two places. They're reorganized around a single Options hub now, so wherever you open your settings from, everything is where you'd expect it.

Under the hood, this one is version 1.0.0.12.
